# WS13 UAT Training and Operating Model

## Objective and Business Outcomes
- Validate production readiness with Corporate Finance Actuarial users and transition to BAU.
- Establish sustainable support and ownership model post go-live.

## In Scope
- UAT planning, execution, and defect management.
- Role-based training for producers, consumers, and stewards.
- BAU support model, SLAs, and operational ownership handover.

## Out of Scope
- Major scope expansion requiring redesign of upstream data products.

## Inputs and Dependencies
- Inputs: WS08 operations outputs, WS09 dashboards, WS10 anomaly workflows.
- Dependencies: business user availability and support team onboarding.

## Detailed Task Backlog (Sequenced)
1. Define UAT scenarios aligned to Corporate Finance Actuarial outcomes.
2. Execute UAT cycles with defect triage and closure criteria.
3. Deliver role-based training sessions and quick-reference guides.
4. Confirm BAU support model with on-call and escalation definitions.
5. Complete handover readiness assessment and sign-off.

## Deliverables
- UAT plan and execution results.
- Training material pack and attendance records.
- BAU operating model and support SLAs.

## RACI-lite
- Accountable: Business readiness lead.
- Responsible: QA lead, enablement lead, and support lead.
- Consulted: Corporate Finance Actuarial champions and FE.
- Informed: governance and executive stakeholders.

## Risks and Mitigations
- Risk: low UAT participation reduces confidence.
  - Mitigation: secure named delegates and schedule protected UAT windows.
- Risk: support model gaps after go-live.
  - Mitigation: run go-live simulation and review escalation readiness.

## Exit Criteria / Acceptance Criteria
- UAT pass criteria achieved with critical defects closed.
- BAU support ownership accepted by operational teams.

## Demo Evidence Required for Sign-off
- UAT evidence review with defect burn-down and support handover checklist.
